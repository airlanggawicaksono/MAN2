# Product

## Register

product

## Users

School admin staff at MAN 2 Yogyakarta (a state Islamic senior high school in Yogyakarta, Indonesia). They sit at a desk during school hours and work through the system in Indonesian. Primary tasks: register and maintain student/teacher profiles, review daily attendance from the RFID desktop app, process leave records, and transition graduating students to alumni. Power users who run the same workflows daily — efficiency and predictability matter more than discoverability.

## Product Purpose

Simandaya is the school management information system (SIS) for MAN 2 Yogyakarta. It pairs with a Flutter desktop app (sijinak) that drives an RFID reader at the gate. Core jobs: manage student and civitas (teacher/staff) data, synchronise attendance records from the gate hardware, handle izin keluar (student leave), and maintain a clean alumni registry separate from active students. Success = zero paper, fast corrections, and reliable daily reports.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, precise, institutional. The tone is formal but never cold — Indonesian public-sector formality softened by clear action cues and honest feedback. Every label is Indonesian; no gratuitous English.

## Anti-references

Generic Bootstrap admin templates (AdminLTE, CoreUI). Consumer SaaS dashboards with big gradient hero metrics. Anything that looks "AI-generated" — identical card grids, glass morphism panels, purple-on-dark gradient text.

## Design Principles

1. Data clarity over decoration — every element earns its place by conveying information or enabling action.
2. Trust through consistency — same patterns across every entity type; admins should never have to relearn.
3. Bulk-first for repetitive work — selecting ranges and applying actions should feel natural, not bolted on.
4. Honest state — loading, empty, error, and success states are never ambiguous.
5. Indonesian first — all copy, labels, and feedback in Indonesian; no mixed-language UI.
